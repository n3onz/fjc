Category A

Your first skydive

Goals of the Category A

This skydive is meant to teach the importance of good body position, heading awareness, and the ability to respond to instructor feedback. Sensory overload is real on your first jump, remain calm and relaxed, hold your arch, and listen to your instructors.

You MUST deploy on your own at the correct altitude to pass this category.

Exit Procedures

You've made it up to altitude and it's time to go. Get ready to skydive and get in the door.
Remember: left hand, left foot, right hand, right foot, keep your hips forward in a good arch.

  • Check in (Wait for the ok)
  • Check out (Wait for the ok)
  • Look up! Up, down, ARCH and OUT...!
  • Hold your arch (head back, hips forward, toes pointed) until stable and start working through the dive flow.
  • If you tumble out of the exit, ARCH (if I could bold this more I would), and hold until stable

Dive Flow

Now that you are stable and with your instructors you will begin the HASP cycle.

  • Heading - Ensure you are not spinning, find a point on the horizon and stay facing that direction.
  • Altitude - Check your altimeter to see if it is time to lock on.
  • Secondary - Check in with your secondary instructor and respond to any hand signals.
  • Primary - Check in with your primary instructor and respond to any hand signals.
  • If you lose one instructor, stick with the other. If you lose both instructors: PULL!

Once your instructors are comfortable they will give you the practice touch signal. You will then execute 3x practice touches.

  • Arch
  • Reach
  • Recover - slowly!

After the completion of your practice touches, continue HASP cycles and stay relaxed down to deployment altitude, lock on, and deploy!

Deployment

Sequence:
  • 6000' - Lock on
  • 5500' - Wave off
  • Arch, reach, deploy
  • 4000,3000,2000,1000 - Check Canopy!

Priorities:
  • You MUST deploy your own parachute!
  • You WILL deploy at the right height
  • You WILL deploy while stable

Landing

Now you're under canopy, look up and ensure that your canopy is shaped and stable, then give a good flare, make a 90 degree turn right and then left to ensure it is steerable. You will then start flying your canopy to your holding area. Once in your holding area, you will fly circles until beginning your landing sequence.

  • 900' - Turn into your pattern
  • 600' - Turn left onto your base leg
  • 300' - Turn left onto your final
  • 10-12' - FLARE, FLARE, FLARE!
  • If you flared too early - Prepare for PLF
  • On the ground - Hold your flare for 3 steps then retrieve your parachute and celebrate!
